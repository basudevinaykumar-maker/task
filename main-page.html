Marine Oil Spill Prevention Initiative

A single oil spill can poison thousands of lives beneath the waves, disrupting fragile marine ecosystems.

Major Oil Spill Impacts

In the BP Oil Spill, more than 200 million gallons of crude oil was pumped into the Gulf of Mexico for 87 days, making it the biggest oil spill in U.S. history.

16,000 total miles of coastline have been affected, including Texas, Louisiana, Mississippi, Alabama, and Florida.

BP is responsible for close to $40 billion in fines, cleanup costs, and settlements due to the 2010 oil spill.

Understanding Oil Spills

What are Oil Spills?

  • An oil spill is the release of liquid petroleum into oceans or rivers.
  • Oil blocks sunlight and oxygen, damaging marine life.
  • Environmental recovery can take decades.

Where Do Oil Spills Come From?

  1. Oil Tanker Accidents
  2. Offshore Oil Drilling Failures
  3. Pipeline Leaks
  4. Human Errors & Illegal Dumping
